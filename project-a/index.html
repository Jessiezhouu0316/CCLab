Mosselini

Scientific Name:

Mossolini

Discovered by / at:

Jessie Zhou, 2025

Family:

Dataflora — digital flora that grows through algorithmic repetition, noise variation and human influence.

Habitat:

The most unmanned place on Earth in the virtual world.

Appearance:

A constantly growing cluster of four-pointed stars and breathes with size when moving. Its hues shift across the spectrum randomly, like living pixels spreading across a digital pond.

Key Characteristics:

  • Mood swings constantly.
  • Vulnerable to human approaches.
  • Forms symbiotic relationships with other digital terrains.
  • Never repeats itself — each individual is unique.

What we know so far about Mosselini? Mosselini is a fictional moss-like organism inspired by fragile ecosystems. It combines natural floating movement with reactive behavior, giving a sense of vulnerability and mood. Observations suggest it thrives best in environments with minimal disturbance, and it reacts instantly to approaching entities by maintaining a safe distance. Its changing colors are not just aesthetic but represent its “emotional” state.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature

  • Approach slowly: sudden movements cause the moss to retreat.
  • Observe without touching: it will darken and slow down if stressed.
  • Let it drift freely: enjoy the changing colors and shapes as it flows naturally.
